Assistant Professor in the <a href="http://www.centralesupelec.fr/en/" target="_blank">CentraleSupélec</a> Graduate School of Engineering at <a href="http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en" target="_blank">Université Paris-Saclay</a>, Paris-Saclay campus, France.

Deputy Head of the Master of Engineering program <a href="http://www.acd-ecp.com" target="_blank">Aménagement et Construction Durables</a> at CentraleSupélec.

Teaching activities in continuum mechanics, structural mechanics, and structural reliability. Currently involved in the design of several new courses within the upcoming new curriculum of CentraleSupélec and a founding member of the <a href="http://..." target="_blank">City Faber Lab</a> that hosts student projects oriented toward modeling Smart City and designing tools for sustainable urban construction.

Research work in the field of computational mechanics for structural reliability in seismic areas with focus on modeling damping in the numerical seismic analysis of nonlinear structures. Research developing within the <a href="http://www.centralesupelec.fr/en/soil-structures-and-materials-mechanics-laboratory-mssmat-umr-cnrs-8579" target="_blank">MSSMat Laboratory</a> (UMR CNRS 8579 & CentraleSupélec) and within the <a href="http://civil.columbia.edu" target="_blank">CEEM Department</a> at Columbia University, NY, USA, with the support of a European <a href="http://ec.europa.eu/research/mariecurieactions/actions/individual-fellowships_en" target="_blank">Marie Sklodowska-Curie Fellowship</a>.
