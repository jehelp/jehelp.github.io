I am an Assistant Professor in the <a href="http://www.centralesupelec.fr/en/" target="_blank">CentraleSupélec</a> Graduate School at <a href="http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en" target="_blank">Université Paris-Saclay</a>, Paris-Saclay campus, France.

I am Deputy Head of the Master of Engineering program <a href="http://www.acd-ecp.com" target="_blank">Aménagement et Construction Durables</a> at CentraleSupélec.

My teaching activities are in continuum mechanics, structural mechanics, and structural reliability. I am currently involved in the design of several new courses within the upcoming new curriculum of CentraleSupélec and a founding member of the <a href="http://..." target="_blank">City Faber Lab</a> that hosts student projects oriented toward modeling Smart City and designing tools for sustainable urban construction.

My research work is in the field of computational mechanics for structural realiability in seismic areas. It focusses on modeling damping in the numerical seismic analysis of nonlinear structures. I have been developing this research within the <a href="http://www.centralesupelec.fr/en/soil-structures-and-materials-mechanics-laboratory-mssmat-umr-cnrs-8579" target="_blank">MSSMat Laboratory</a> (UMR CNRS 8579 & CentraleSupélec) and within the <a href="http://civil.columbia.edu" target="_blank">CEEM Department</a> at Columbia University, NY, USA, which I joined with the support of a European <a href="http://ec.europa.eu/research/mariecurieactions/actions/individual-fellowships_en" target="_blank">Marie Sklodowska-Curie Fellowship</a>.
